Intro

I like puzzles. A lot. I like solving them, and I also like making them. One of my favorite events each year is the MIT Mystery Hunt, a huge, weekend-long puzzle competition. In the Mystery Hunt, each puzzle gives a word or phrase, and the answers to several otherwise-unrelated puzzle can be combined to form a meta-puzzle. These puzzles don't necessarily have instructions, or at least not complete instructions; figuring out how to interpret what you have, or even where to begin, is usually part of the fun. I've posted general advice and some specific hints for these puzzles here.

Most of the time, a meta-puzzle includes the set-up for a joke of some sort, with the answer being the punchline. With that in mind...
